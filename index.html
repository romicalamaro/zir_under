Topkapı Scroll

The Topkapı Scroll is a late-fifteenth- or early-sixteenth-century Timurid pattern scroll held in the library of the Topkapı Palace Museum in Istanbul. It gathers 114 geometric designs — known as girih, or “knot,” patterns — that served as a working reference for the architects and craftsmen who covered walls, domes, and muqarnas vaults across the Persian and Islamic world. Rather than written instructions, the scroll records the patterns themselves, showing how a small set of modular tiles could be repeated and interlaced to build endlessly complex ornament. It remains one of the very few surviving sources that reveal how this visual language of geometry was conceived, recorded, and passed from one generation of makers to the next.
